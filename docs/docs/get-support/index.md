# Get Support

If you or someone you know needs assistance, you're not alone. Our community network is here to help.

::: warning YOUR PRIVACY MATTERS
We collect only the minimum information needed to help you. We do not keep records of who we help. Your safety is our priority.
:::

## Types of Support Available

Depending on your local cell's capacity, support may include:

| Support Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Transportation** | Safe rides to work, appointments, grocery stores, court dates |
| **Know Your Rights** | Training on your legal rights during encounters |
| **Legal Connections** | Referrals to immigration attorneys and legal aid |
| **Emergency Resources** | Food, supplies, and emergency funds |
| **Family Support** | Help with childcare, school coordination if a family member is detained |

## How to Request Support

### If You Know Someone in the Network

The safest way to connect is through someone you already know and trust who is part of a local cell. Ask them to connect you with the appropriate support.

### If You Don't Know Anyone

Use the form below to reach out. A community member will contact you through a secure channel.

## What Happens Next

1. A community member will contact you through your preferred method
2. They will confirm your needs and connect you with the right support
3. All communication will be through secure channels
4. You will never be asked for documentation or proof of status

## Emergency Situations

**If you or someone is being detained RIGHT NOW:**
- Remain calm
- Say: "I am exercising my right to remain silent. I want an attorney."
- Do not sign anything
- Call [your cell's emergency legal line] or a local immigration attorney immediately

**National Immigration Legal Hotlines:**
- United We Dream: 1-844-363-1423
- RAICES: 1-888-305-4945
